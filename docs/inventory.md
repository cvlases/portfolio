## Newspeak Dictionary
**Category:** Build + Art (cross-listed on art page as visual)
**Hook:** I wanted to find the words AI had hollowed out. What I found instead was that the literal meanings were already inside the model — waiting for the right literary framing to call them back.
**Role:** Solo developer / artist
**Team:** Solo (Advanced Creative Code course capstone, CMU)
**Time:** Spring 2025
**Tools:** Next.js, Modal Labs, Gemma-2-2B, GemmaScope, SAE feature steering, GPT-2

**What it is:**
A web experience exploring how AI language models handle words with deep concrete natural-world meanings — words like "soil," "root," "bloom." The project uses SAE feature steering and prompt engineering to probe what the model actually knows versus what it defaults to saying. The thesis shifted during development: started as "AI has hollowed these words out" and became "the literal meanings are already inside the model and can be invoked through the right literary framing."

Vintage-glitch aesthetic. Deployed on Modal Labs with Next.js frontend.

**Process:**
- Concept: Started from observation that AI prose flattens words with physical/sensory weight
- Technical: GPT-2 / GemmaScope exploration → moved to Gemma-2-2B with SAE feature steering
- Thesis shift: The pivot from "AI hollowed these words" to "the meanings are already there" — this is the most interesting part of the story
- Aesthetic: Vintage-glitch visual language, feels like finding something buried

## Pittsburgh / Mon Valley Pollution Analysis
**Category:** Build + Research (cross-listed) + Planet (cross-listed)
**Hook:** Community smell reports and industrial pollution models are measuring the same thing from opposite ends. When you cross-reference them, a picture emerges that neither dataset shows alone.
**Role:** Research assistant / data analyst
**Team:** CREATE Lab, CMU
**Time:** [FILL IN]
**Tools:** Python, Jupyter, GeoTIFF rasters, [FILL IN — geopandas? folium?]

**What it is:**
Analysis of industrial dispersion model GeoTIFF rasters for pollution monitoring in the Pittsburgh / Mon Valley region, cross-referenced with SmellPGH community odor reports. Key findings: weak-to-moderate correlation between regional pollution sum and smell reports (suggesting they're complementary signals, not redundant), and regional max outperforms regional sum for predicting community-felt impact.

**Process:**
- Data: Dispersion model rasters + SmellPGH community report database
- Analysis: Correlation between modeled pollution and reported smells
- Key insight: Regional max > regional sum for community impact prediction
- Implication: Community sensing fills gaps that instruments miss

## GenAIxRad
**Category:** Research + Build (cross-listed)
**Hook:** Radiologists using AI diagnostic tools face a problem nobody had named yet: how do you ask a system what it found when you don't know what to ask? We called it the selection problem.
**Role:** Co-researcher, co-author
**Team:** Co-authored with Katelyn Morrison (CMU)
**Time:** 2024–2025
**Tools:** Gemini 2.5 Flash (orchestrator), OHIF clinical CT viewer, multi-agent architecture, [FILL IN — Python? LangChain?]

**What it is:**
A multi-agent XAI system embedded in the OHIF clinical CT viewer. Gemini 2.5 Flash acts as an orchestrator with four specialized agents. The paper formalizes "the selection problem" — the difficulty radiologists face in querying AI systems about findings when they don't yet know what findings exist. Submitted to IJCAI 2026.

**Contributions:**
- Formalizing "the selection problem" as a named construct
- Query taxonomy
- Failure mode taxonomy
- System architecture (orchestrator + 4 specialized agents)
