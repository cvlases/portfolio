// advocacy lens

What this work taught me about institutions, systems, public impact, and the rest of my practice.

Systems, not slogans

Climate work taught me to think at the level of incentives, policy, infrastructure, and long-term consequences—not just individual behavior change.

Public-interest technology

It made me care deeply about who technology serves, who absorbs the risk, and what responsibility looks like when stakes are collective.

Why it connects to my work

This is part of why I care so much about ethics, governance, research, and building tools that hold up in the real world—not just in theory.

Research overlap

Environmental work sharpened how I frame messy systems problems and ask better questions about who is affected.

Ethics overlap

It is a direct reason I care about responsible AI, governance, and public-interest technology.

Design overlap

It taught me to think in terms of stakeholders, tradeoffs, and consequences—not just users and features.
